Penn State School of Music faculty to perform April 30 in Boalsburg

Thursday, February 17, 2011
Internationally renowned cellist and Penn State’s first laureate Kim Cook will join headline musicians performing at 'Music at the Boal Mansion' on April 30, at the Boal Mansion Museum in Boalsburg, Pa.

Members of the faculty in Penn State's School of Music will be featured during the "Music at the Boal Mansion" spring concert and reception at 8 p.m. on April 30, in Boalsburg, Pa.

Organized by Penn State professor and clarinetist Anthony Costa, musical selections will range from those from early 19th Century masters Chopin and Mendelssohn to recent works from Kye Ryung Park (born 1974) and Paul Chiara (born 1938). The concert also will feature selections from Luigi Cherubini (a composer much-admired by Beethoven) Jacques Ibert, a 20th Century French composer.

Performers from the Penn State School of Music faculty will include internationally renowned cellist and the University's inaugural Penn State Laureate, Kim Cook, who was also an international artistic ambassador for the U.S. Department of State; pianist Enrico Elisi, winner of the top awards at the Venice Competition and at the Oporto, Portugal, International Competition; and a host of accomplished voice, string and woodwind faculty led by clarinetist Costa.

The reception will feature fine food and drink; tickets are $75 a person and benefit both historic preservation and Penn State's Music at Penn's Woods event in June. For reservations, contact the Boal Mansion Museum at 814-466-6210 or office@boalmuseum.com. For details, visit http://boalmuseum.com/ online. The Boal Mansion Museum is located at 163 Boal Estate Dr., Boalsburg, Pa.
