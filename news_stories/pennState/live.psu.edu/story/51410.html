New Kensington campus continues its Year on Spain celebration

Wednesday, February 16, 2011

Penn State New Kensington continues its International Year on Spain at 2 p.m. on Thursday, Feb. 24, with a showing of "Carmen" in the Forum Theatre.

The 1983 movie, directed and by Carlos Saura, is an adaptation of the novel by Prosper Mérimée, using music from the opera by Georges Bizet. Choreographed in the flamenco style of dance, a cross-cultural form that originated with people in southern Spain, the film is a story of a middle-aged choreographer and his unrequited love for the lead dancer during rehearsal of a stage production of "Carmen." Saura's version was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Foreign Film and won a British Academy of Film and television Arts (BAFTA) for Best Foreign Language Film. The event is free to the public.

The campus designated the 2010-11 school year as the "International Year on Spain." Throughout the fall and spring semesters, students and faculty have explored and reflected on various aspects of Spain's history and economic, social and political reality. Special events celebrated the culture and heritage of the country. The Saura film, one of more than 35 on the subject, complements a flamenco dance performance last semester by Centro Flamenco de Pittsburgh.

The study of Spain culminates in March when faculty, staff and alumni experience a week of Spanish culture. Arranged by New Kensington's international program directors -- Bill Hamilton, assistant professor of biology, and Maria Franco de Gomez, instructor in Spanish -- the group will visit Barcelona, Granada, Seville and Madrid.

The global travel program was established by Hamilton and Franco de Gomez to give the campus’ students the opportunity to directly experience other countries and other cultures. The program provides students with a variety of opportunities to study in foreign countries for varying periods of time — week, summer, semester or full year. The objective of the program is to offer students a broader education than can be obtained through classroom experiences.

Penn State New Kensington has embarked on the promotion of greater awareness and understanding of world issues, international trends and global policy debates. Each year, the campus adopts a country or region of the world to inspire teaching and scholarship. Students, faculty and staff will explore and reflect on various aspects of Spain's history, culture and economic, social and political reality.

Since 2004, more than 130 campus students have traveled to Spain, France, Italy, China, Greece, Peru and the Galapagos Islands. In conjunction with the trips, students are required to take a related course that enriches their understanding of the destination and its culture. Upon return, participants present their experience through narratives and slideshows to the entire campus community. These presentations both enable others to share in the immediate travel experience and help to generate excitement in students, as well as faculty and staff, to become involved in future trips.

For more about global programs, visit http://www.nk.psu.edu/Academics/InternationalPrograms/intlprograms.htm online.
