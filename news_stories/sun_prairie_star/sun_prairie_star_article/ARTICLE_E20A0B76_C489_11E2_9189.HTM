Depression symptoms 0 comments

Depression may involve the following symptoms, but must include the feelings of hopelessness and lack of interest in activities you once enjoyed. Symptoms must be present most of the day and last for prolonged period of at least two weeks.

You may be experiencing depression if you have:

• Changes in sleep habits, difficulty falling asleep or sleeping excessively;

• Changes in eating habits. Most cases involve decreased appetite, while still others may experience an increased appetite;

• Poor concentration, an inability to maintain focus, struggle to concentrate while watching a 30-minute TV program or follow the story in a news article;

• Lack of energy, having trouble maintaining daily chores and interest in activities;

• Low self-esteem. People with depression dwell on guilt, failure and loss, accompanied by feelings of hopelessness; and

• Loss of energy and mood swings. Those with depression have trouble with physical ailments, and are slow to move physically. Mood swings may also occur. The depressed person might be easily aggravated.

For more information contact the National Alliance on Mental Illness at www.nami.org.
